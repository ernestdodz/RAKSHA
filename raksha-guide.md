# Raksha: Board MOBA Tactical Strategy Arena - Comprehensive Game Guide

## 1. Game Summary
Raksha is a deterministic turn-based board battler where movement, duels, skill objects, shrine control, and positioning decide each match. It is set in a post-Crystalline Collapse world where gods are summoned to prevent reality from splitting further, bargaining with steel, thunder, stone, and oath. The game features a conflict between Order and Chaos, with various gods representing different aspects of this struggle [1].

## 2. How to Play
Players take one action per turn, choosing between **Move**, **Attack**, or **Skill**. Moving involves one tile in any of the 8 directions. When a duel is declared, the board determines the outcome. Gods do not have HP bars; instead, a fallen god returns to its home side and must re-enter the fight. Skills leave marks on the field, influencing future movement and strategy [1].

## 3. Key Mechanics
*   **God Hero**: Your main piece, capable of movement, attacking, and placing skills [1].
*   **Altar**: Your stronghold. Its destruction leads to an immediate round loss [1].
*   **HP**: Each side starts with a fixed life total. Losing a god in a duel typically costs 1 HP, unless a blessing prevents it [1].
*   **Shrines**: Two shrines, Order and Chaos, exist on the board. Channeling a shrine requires standing on it for one full turn. A blessing activates at the start of your next turn if you remain on the shrine. Leaving early cancels channeling. Shrines start charged and cool down after a blessing is spent [1].
    *   **Order Blessing**: Wards off the first life loss from a defeated duel on that turn [1].
    *   **Chaos Blessing**: Tilts your next initiated attack duel in your favor [1].
*   **Skill Object**: A placed skill that remains on the board, altering movement through that space [1].
*   **Chaordic Event / Chaordic Pressure**: If Order and Chaos remain in equilibrium for too long, or if HP stays tied, the battlefield destabilizes and contracts inward. This acts as a late-game safety system to prevent stalemates [1].

## 4. Win Conditions
There are three primary ways to achieve victory in Raksha [1]:
1.  **Altar Defeat**: Attack the enemy altar and win the duel for an instant win. This is often the fastest win condition if a clear lane to the altar is available [1].
2.  **Player HP**: Each side begins with a set HP. If one of your gods falls in a duel and returns, your side loses 1 HP (unless a blessing intervenes). If your HP reaches 0, that player immediately loses [1].
3.  **Chaordic System**: If the match remains tied for too long, or both sides repeat the same battle rhythm, the Chaordic system will collapse the arena, leading to a loss for the player whose HP reaches 0 first [1].

## 5. Active Heroes

### Anika: Mirrorbound Huntress
*   **Skill: Clone**: Summon one clone beside Anika; it auto-acts after Anika acts. **Tip:** Play aggressively with Anika to trigger constant clone pressure [2].
*   **Lore**: Anika learned the old shadow rite, fighting with reflected selves as sworn echoes. She is known for her aggressive playstyle and reopening the East Causeway. Inspired by Chhaya in Hindu myth and warrior-oath traditions [2].

### Faros: Tempest of the Burning Straits
*   **Skill: Cyclone**: Place a cyclone trap; adjacent enemies are pushed away when it triggers. **Tip:** Place along choke paths to disrupt enemy altar approach [3].
*   **Lore**: Faros rose from reef cities, invoking wind-lords. He believes controlled devastation can prevent another quiet extinction. Inspired by Aeolus and Mediterranean storm-lord motifs, fused with lighthouse-warden legends [3].

### Jumka: Willow Matron
*   **Skill: Willow**: Summon autonomous willow spirits that pressure nearby enemies. **Tip:** Use willows as a defensive wall while your second hero advances [4].
*   **Lore**: Jumka learned from grove myths, planting willows across crater fields. She defends Raksha because unchecked Chaos turns mourning into desert. Inspired by dryad and sacred-tree traditions across Greek and East Asian folklore [4].

### Kidu: Spark Runner
*   **Skill: Zap**: Place zap marks and teleport Kidu onto owned zap tiles. **Tip:** Chain zap marks toward enemy altar for rapid re-engage paths [5].
*   **Lore**: Kidu was born in thunder seasons, remaking legacy as speed. His zap network is both weapon and philosophy: never meet a fortress where it expects you. Inspired by Enkidu's untamed force from Mesopotamian epic and storm-messenger archetypes [5].

### Mahui: Pillar Warden
*   **Skill: Pillar**: Place a pillar trap; adjacent enemies are eliminated when it triggers. **Tip:** Layer pillars near your altar lanes to punish direct rushes [6].
*   **Lore**: Mahui carries stories of the hook-bearing demigod who raised islands from the deep. He remains on the board because fortification is more than defense. Inspired by Maui's world-shaping feats in Polynesian traditions and basalt-guardian folklore [6].

### Sajik: Dune Veil Binder
*   **Skill: Quicksand**: Place quicksand; adjacent enemies are pulled into the tile on trigger. **Tip:** Set quicksand where enemies are forced to path through [7].
*   **Lore**: Sajik was raised by caravan mystics, learning that terrain itself can be persuaded to betray the overconfident. His quicksand rites rewrite momentum, dragging attackers into positions their own plans cannot survive. Inspired by desert deity traditions around Set/Sutekh and sand-binding djinn folklore [7].

## 6. Game Modes
Raksha offers several game modes, including Player vs Player (PvP), Player vs Bot (PvB), Bot vs Bot (BvB), and the ability to watch live battles. The game can be played on different board sizes: Tactical 9x9 and Strategical 13x13 [1].
